Clinical symptoms
SMA type I also called Werdnig-Hoffmann disease or infantile-onset SMA- hypotonia (reduced muscle tone) -(before 6 months of age) diminished limb movements, lack of tendon reflexes, fasciculations, swallowing and feeding difficulties, scoliosis and impaired breathing.
SMA type II-(between 6 and 18 months of age) the intermediate form- unable to stand or walk unaided.
SMA type III (Kugelberg-Welander disease)- (after 18 months of age) coliosis and joint contractures—chronic shortening of muscles or tendons around joints--caused by abnormal muscle tone and weakness.
SMA type IV-(after 21 years of age) with mild to moderate proximal muscle weakness and other symptoms.
Symptoms, PCR for exon 7 and 8 deletion, MLPA
